Symptoms of Osteoarthritis & Spondylosis

Do you experience any of these signs?

Chronic Joint & Back Pain

Persistent pain in the knees, Neck, or lower back. The pain typically gets worse with activity or prolonged standing and feels better after rest.

Morning Stiffness & Rigidity

A "locked" feeling in the joints. Stiffness is most severe in the morning or after sitting for a long time, making it hard to get moving.

Grating Sensation (Crepitus)

A noticeable crunching or crackling sound (crepitus) when moving the knee or neck. This indicates cartilage wear and bone rubbing against bone.

Numbness & Tingling

A "pins and needles" sensation, numbness, or weakness in the arms, hands, legs, or feet. This is often caused by nerve compression in the spine.

Loss of Flexibility

Significantly reduced range of motion. You may find it difficult to bend your knees, turn your neck, or perform daily movements like walking.

Joint Swelling & Weakness

Soft swelling around the affected joints or hard bony growths (spurs). This is often accompanied by muscle weakness around the knee or spine.

Recognize these symptoms? Don't delay diagnosis.
